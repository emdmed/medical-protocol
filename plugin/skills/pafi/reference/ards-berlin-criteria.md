# ARDS Berlin Definition (2012)

## Diagnostic Criteria (ALL required)

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Timing** | Within 1 week of known clinical insult or new/worsening respiratory symptoms |
| **Imaging** | Bilateral opacities on chest X-ray or CT — not fully explained by effusions, atelectasis, or nodules |
| **Origin of edema** | Respiratory failure not fully explained by cardiac failure or fluid overload (objective assessment needed if no risk factor) |
| **Oxygenation** | PaO₂/FiO₂ ratio with minimum PEEP of 5 cmH₂O |

## Severity Classification

| Severity | PaO₂/FiO₂ | PEEP Requirement | Mortality |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Mild | 201–300 mmHg | ≥ 5 cmH₂O | 27% |
| Moderate | 101–200 mmHg | ≥ 5 cmH₂O | 32% |
| Severe | ≤ 100 mmHg | ≥ 5 cmH₂O | 45% |

## Important Notes

- **PEEP ≥ 5 cmH₂O is mandatory** for classification — readings without PEEP do not qualify
- **FiO₂ must be documented** alongside PaO₂ for valid ratio calculation
- **SpO₂/FiO₂ ratio** can be used as surrogate when ABG is unavailable (SpO₂/FiO₂ < 315 ≈ PaO₂/FiO₂ < 300)
- **Re-classify** at 24 hours — initial severity may change with treatment
