# Acid-Base Compensation Formulas

## Metabolic Acidosis

| Type | Expected pCO₂ |
|------|---------------|
| Acute | pCO₂ = (1.5 × HCO₃⁻) + 8 ± 2 (Winter's formula) |
| Chronic | Not typically applicable — metabolic acidosis is usually acute |

**Interpretation:** If measured pCO₂ is lower than expected → concurrent respiratory alkalosis. If higher → concurrent respiratory acidosis.

## Metabolic Alkalosis

| Expected pCO₂ |
|---------------|
| pCO₂ = (0.7 × HCO₃⁻) + 21 ± 2 |
| Alternative: pCO₂ rises ~0.7 mmHg per 1 mEq/L rise in HCO₃⁻ |

## Respiratory Acidosis

| Type | Expected HCO₃⁻ Change |
|------|----------------------|
| Acute | ↑ 1 mEq/L per 10 mmHg rise in pCO₂ |
| Chronic | ↑ 3.5 mEq/L per 10 mmHg rise in pCO₂ |

## Respiratory Alkalosis

| Type | Expected HCO₃⁻ Change |
|------|----------------------|
| Acute | ↓ 2 mEq/L per 10 mmHg fall in pCO₂ |
| Chronic | ↓ 5 mEq/L per 10 mmHg fall in pCO₂ |

## Quick Reference

- Normal pH: 7.35–7.45
- Normal pCO₂: 35–45 mmHg
- Normal HCO₃⁻: 22–26 mEq/L
