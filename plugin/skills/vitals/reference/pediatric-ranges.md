# Pediatric Vital Sign Ranges

## By Age Group

### Neonate (0–28 days)
| Vital Sign | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Heart Rate | 100–160 bpm |
| Respiratory Rate | 30–60 breaths/min |
| Systolic BP | 60–90 mmHg |
| Temperature | 36.5–37.5°C |
| SpO₂ | > 95% (> 90% in first minutes of life) |

### Infant (1–12 months)
| Vital Sign | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Heart Rate | 100–150 bpm |
| Respiratory Rate | 25–40 breaths/min |
| Systolic BP | 70–100 mmHg |
| Temperature | 36.5–37.5°C |

### Toddler (1–3 years)
| Vital Sign | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Heart Rate | 90–140 bpm |
| Respiratory Rate | 20–30 breaths/min |
| Systolic BP | 80–110 mmHg |

### Child (4–12 years)
| Vital Sign | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Heart Rate | 70–120 bpm |
| Respiratory Rate | 16–24 breaths/min |
| Systolic BP | 85–120 mmHg |

### Adolescent (13–17 years)
| Vital Sign | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Heart Rate | 60–100 bpm |
| Respiratory Rate | 12–20 breaths/min |
| Systolic BP | 90–130 mmHg |

## Key Differences from Adults

- Pediatric heart rates and respiratory rates are **higher** than adults
- Blood pressure is **lower** and increases with age
- **Formula for minimum systolic BP:** 70 + (2 × age in years) mmHg
- Temperature regulation is less mature in neonates — hypothermia risk is higher
