Common questions

Straight answers, no jargon.

"I don't code — is this really for me?"

That's exactly the point. You describe what you need in clinical language — "I need a vital signs monitor for my trauma patients" — and it's built for you. If something isn't right, you describe what to change. You never see code.

"Is patient data safe?"

All data stays on your computer. There's no cloud database, no account, no login. The only thing that touches the internet is your conversation with Claude — and you should never paste patient data into that. Enter it directly into the interfaces.

"How is this different from MDCalc?"

MDCalc gives you one calculator at a time. Medical Protocol builds integrated clinical interfaces — your vitals feed into pulse oximetry, your ABG analyzer sits next to fluid balance, and everything lives in one dashboard customized to your workflow.

"What if it builds something wrong?"

Every clinical calculation is verified by 527 unit tests covering validated medical logic — Winter's formula, anion gap, vital sign ranges, BMI categories, ASCVD risk. If something doesn't look right, tell Claude and it fixes it immediately.

"Do I need IT approval?"

Not for your personal laptop. It runs locally and stores data only on your machine. If your department later wants to adopt it officially, the architecture makes it straightforward to integrate with institutional systems.

"What does it cost?"

You need a Claude Code subscription from Anthropic. The medical interfaces themselves are free and open source. Think of it like a tool that understands medicine — the subscription is for the tool, not the medical features.
